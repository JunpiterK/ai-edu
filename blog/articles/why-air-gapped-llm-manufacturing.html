Why manufacturers must run LLMs air-gapped

AE AI Edu Archive Semiconductor process engineer · Air-gapped LLM / RAG / Agents

The first time I proposed putting an LLM inside the fab, security killed it in ten minutes — and they were right. I'd sketched a slick little assistant that would answer process questions from a hosted API, and I hadn't thought for one second about where the question itself would go. The reviewer asked one thing: "So the deposition recipe leaves the building the moment someone types it?" I didn't have an answer. That meeting is why I'm writing this.

Here's what took me too long to internalize: in a fab or a process plant, the deposition parameters, the yield-vs-defect curves, the failure signatures your veterans read at a glance — that undocumented know-how is the company. And the uncomfortable part of adopting AI is that the moment an engineer pastes a real process question into a cloud LLM, that know-how has already left. So the thing I got wrong up front, and eventually got right, is the whole point of this piece: for manufacturers, the model has to come to the data, not the data to the model. 제조업에서 노하우는 곧 회사이고, 클라우드 LLM 프롬프트는 그 노하우가 나가는 문이라는 걸 저는 저 회의에서 처음 제대로 깨달았습니다.

What actually leaves when you paste into a cloud LLM

"Just try ChatGPT on it" feels harmless because the box looks like a search bar. It isn't, and I learned the shape of the problem by watching a well-meaning colleague do it. He pasted a real excursion write-up — tool ID, the recipe step, the actual defect numbers — into a public model to get help wording a summary. No malice, genuinely trying to move fast. But every character of that prompt is an outbound transfer to infrastructure none of us control, and once it's out, it's out. Here's the exposure I now walk people through, in the order it bit us:

  • The trade secret is the prompt. To get a useful answer you have to describe the real problem — the recipe, the tool, the numbers. The context that makes the answer good is the exact context you can't afford to send. You can't have one without the other, and I kept underestimating that.
  • Yield & defect data is competitive intelligence. A defect-rate table or an excursion timeline tells a competitor precisely where your process is soft. That's not something you leak on purpose — you leak it one "quick question" at a time.
  • The NDA doesn't care that it "was just a chatbot." Customer NDAs, and in a lot of sectors export-control rules, draw no line between "emailed the file" and "pasted it into a model." Both are disclosure to a third party. I've had a legal review make exactly that point, and there's no arguing it.
  • Once it crosses a border, you've lost the thread. The moment bytes land in a cloud region, you often can't say where they live, who can subpoena them, or whether they're quietly informing someone else's system.

None of this needs a villain. The ordinary, well-meaning use of a cloud LLM is the leak — that's the part that's hard to police, because nobody feels like they did anything wrong. 악의가 있어야 새는 게 아닙니다. 선의로 쓴 한 번의 질의에 민감정보·수율 데이터·NDA 대상 정보가 그대로 나가더군요.

흔한 실수 ①: "로컬로 돌리니까 데이터는 안 나가겠지."

❌ 로컬 LLM만 세우면 무조건 폐쇄라고 믿는 것. → 저도 처음 로컬 스택을 세우고 안심했는데, 뒤늦게 tcpdump를 걸어보니 인퍼런스 엔진이 아니라 그 옆에 붙인 벡터 DB 클라이언트가 익명 텔레메트리를 밖으로 쏘고 있더군요(Chroma의 PostHog 텔레메트리가 커뮤니티에서 반복적으로 보고되는 대표 사례입니다). 모델은 조용한데 라이브러리가 전화를 걸고 있던 겁니다. → ✅ 해결은 단순합니다: 스택 전체를 올린 뒤 loopback·API 포트를 제외하고 패킷을 실제로 캡처해 아무것도 안 나가는지 눈으로 확인하고, 임베딩·DB 라이브러리의 텔레메트리 옵트아웃(예: 텔레메트리 비활성 플래그)을 명시적으로 끕니다. "안 나갈 것이다"는 검증이 아니라 가정입니다.

The data that makes a prompt worth asking is the same data you can never afford to send.

The alternative: the model comes in, the data stays

The fix that finally cleared that same security review wasn't "don't use LLMs." It was to flip the direction of travel. Instead of shipping your data out to a hosted model, you carry an open-weight model in and serve it inside the fence. When I redrew the architecture that way, the reviewer's objection just evaporated — there was no longer an outbound path to argue about. The whole pattern fits on one diagram, and it's three pieces:

  • On-prem serving. An open-weight model (Llama, Qwen, gpt-oss, and peers) running on your own GPUs behind a serving stack like vLLM or Ollama. The weights are files you copied in once; nothing calls home.
  • Air-gapped RAG. Your SOPs, spec sheets, and equipment logs are embedded and indexed locally, so answers are grounded in your own documents without those documents ever leaving.
  • Internal wiring. The model connects to in-house systems (MES, historians) through audited tools — reads and acts inside the boundary.

흔한 실수 ②: "그냥 pip install하고 모델 받으면 되겠지."

❌ 인터넷 있는 개발 환경에서 짠 코드를 그대로 폐쇄망에 들고 들어가는 것. → 이게 폐쇄망 입문자가 가장 크게 데이는 지점이고, 저도 여기서 하루를 날렸습니다. 진짜 에어갭 안에서는 pip도, huggingface 자동 다운로드도 전부 죽습니다. 코드가 런타임에 몰래 모델 카드나 토크나이저를 받으러 나가려다 조용히 멈추거나 타임아웃 나는 걸, 배포 당일에야 발견하죠. → ✅ 해결: 모든 가중치·의존성을 반입 전에 미리 받아 사내 미러/오프라인 캐시로 옮기고, HF_HUB_OFFLINE=1 같은 오프라인 모드를 강제해 런타임에 밖으로 나가는 경로 자체를 없앱니다. 반입은 해시 검증된 단방향 매체로 한 번만. 커뮤니티의 에어갭 구축 가이드가 하나같이 강조하는 게 이 "미리 다 받아두기"입니다.

The whole thing collapses to one sentence: the model comes in; the data never goes out. Every query — the question, the retrieved context, the answer, even the logs — completes one full cycle inside the air-gapped boundary and never crosses it. And here's the practical payoff I didn't expect: that single closed loop is also the exact picture you hand to a security reviewer. The first version of my design got picked apart because I couldn't draw the boundary; this one gets approved because the boundary is the drawing. 모델은 반입되고, 데이터·프롬프트·로그는 폐쇄망 경계를 한 번도 넘지 않습니다 — 감사 나가서 그릴 그림이 이거 하나로 끝납니다.

Air-gapped means weaker? Not anymore

The objection I hear every single time — usually from the person who wanted the hosted model — is that going on-prem means settling for a dumber one. A few years ago I'd have half-agreed with you. Not now. For grounded, document-based work — the retrieval-heavy Q&A that is honestly 90% of what a plant actually asks — a well-served open-weight model is more than enough, and I've watched skeptical engineers stop caring about the leaderboard once the answers started landing. The shift that made it real is BYO-model (bring your own model): open families like Llama, Qwen, and gpt-oss ship weights you run yourself, so capability is something you install once, not something you rent per token forever.

And here's the part people miss because they're staring at benchmarks: the plant-specific advantage runs the other way. A cloud model has never seen your equipment logs and never will; an air-gapped RAG system reads them directly. A slightly bigger model guessing about your process loses to a smaller one that's actually grounded in your sealed documents — every time. 폐쇄망이라 성능이 떨어진다는 건 옛말입니다. 오픈 모델(Llama·Qwen·gpt-oss)로 충분하고, 제 경험상 사내 문서에 근거한 답이 더 큰 모델의 추측보다 오히려 셉니다.

흔한 실수 ③: "VRAM이 24GB니까 이 모델 들어가겠네."

❌ 모델 가중치 크기만 보고 GPU 용량을 계산하는 것. → r/LocalLLaMA 같은 데서 가장 흔히 보이는 착각인데, 저도 짧은 컨텍스트로 멀쩡히 돌던 세팅이 긴 문서를 붙이자 KV 캐시가 폭발하며 3am에 OOM으로 뻗는 걸 겪었습니다. 컨텍스트가 길어지면 KV 캐시가 모델 가중치보다 더 큰 메모리를 먹습니다(128K에선 가중치의 몇 배). "8B니까 8GB면 되겠지"가 아니라 "8GB면 8B를 컨텍스트 몇 K까지"로 생각해야 하더군요. → ✅ 해결: 실제로 쓸 동시성·컨텍스트 길이로 사이징하고, 모자라면 컨텍스트를 줄이거나 KV 캐시를 INT8로 양자화합니다.

흔한 실수 ④: "양자화? 4비트로 최대한 줄이면 이득이지."

❌ VRAM 아끼겠다고 무턱대고 낮은 비트로 조이는 것. → 표준 후처리 양자화로 4비트까지 내리면 반올림 과정에서 추론·논리 정확도가 예상보다 뚝 떨어지는 경우가 있습니다. 벤치 점수만 보고 넘겼다가 실제 답 품질에서 데이는 전형적 함정이죠. → ✅ 제 기준은 Q4_K_M 정도가 크기 대비 품질 균형점이고, 그 아래로는 반드시 내 문서로 품질을 재보고 결정합니다. 리더보드가 아니라 우리 RAG 답으로.

A first-pass checklist for engineers

You don't need to solve everything to start. But here's the short list I now walk before any pilot — every item on it is something I, or someone whose war story I trust, got wrong at least once:

  • Model-import procedure — and the license. Weights enter once, through a controlled one-way path (hash-verified media / a reviewed inbound gate), nothing pulling from the internet at runtime. But read the license before you build on the model, not after. "Open-weight" is not "open source": several popular families ship community licenses with real strings — usage thresholds, competitor and field-of-use restrictions, bans on training other models. Getting that flagged by legal after you've shipped is a bad day.
  • GPU sizing, for real concurrency. Size to your actual concurrency and context length, not a headline number — and remember the KV cache, not just the weights (see the OOM story above). A single modern data-center GPU already runs a capable mid-size open model.
  • A hard no-egress boundary. The serving host has no route out. Verify it with a packet capture — assume, and you don't have an air gap, you have a hope.
  • Access control & audit. Who can query, which documents each role may retrieve, and a log of every call. The closed loop only counts if it's also an accountable one.
  • Data classification up front. Decide what's allowed into the RAG index before you index it, not after someone asks an awkward question.

흔한 실수 ⑤: "라이브러리 다 깔았는데 왜 안 뜨지" — CUDA/드라이버 지옥.

❌ 폐쇄망 서버의 드라이버·CUDA 버전을 확인 안 하고 서빙 스택을 올리는 것. → vLLM 같은 서빙 엔진의 GitHub 이슈가 이걸로 도배돼 있고(CUDA 12로 빌드된 게 13 위에서 libcudart.so.12 못 찾고 죽거나, 특정 NVIDIA 드라이버가 --tensor-parallel-size를 통째로 깨뜨리는 등), 저도 멀쩡한 코드가 폐쇄망 서버에서만 안 떠서 반나절을 태웠습니다. → ✅ 해결: 드라이버·CUDA·서빙 엔진 버전을 사전에 못 박아 검증하고, 멀티 GPU면 tensor-parallel 세팅이 실제 가시 GPU 수와 맞는지부터 확인. 폐쇄망에선 "일단 최신으로 업글"이 안 되니 버전을 처음부터 고정하는 게 답입니다.

흔한 실수 ⑥: RAG는 세웠는데 검색이 헛도는 것 — 임베딩 언어 미스매치.

❌ 영어권 예제 그대로 영어 위주 임베딩 모델을 쓰고 한국어 SOP를 인덱싱하는 것. → 문서는 한국어인데 임베딩이 한국어를 제대로 못 잡으면, LLM이 아무리 좋아도 엉뚱한 걸 물어옵니다("답이 이상하다"의 범인이 사실 검색 단계인 경우가 많죠). → ✅ 해결: bge-m3처럼 한국어 포함 다국어 검색이 검증된 임베딩을 쓰고, 반드시 실제 우리 문서로 검색 품질을 눈으로 확인한 뒤 넘어갑니다.
