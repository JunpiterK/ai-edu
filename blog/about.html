Our mission

AI Edu Archive exists to help manufacturers do one hard thing well: take the process know-how that lives in their plant — SOPs, spec sheets, equipment logs, and the undocumented judgment of senior engineers — and turn it into an on-prem LLM, RAG, and agent system where not a single byte ever leaves the network.

AI Edu Archive는 제조업의 노하우와 공정 지식을, 데이터가 외부로 나가지 않는 폐쇄형 LLM/RAG/Agent로 체계화하는 방법을 실전 코드와 함께 다룹니다. 화려한 데모가 아니라, 실제 폐쇄망 안에서 감사(audit)를 통과하고 운영되는 시스템을 목표로 합니다.

This is not an AI news blog. It's a working archive for the specific, awkward problem of running modern language-model systems air-gapped — where "just call the API" is not an option, and the value of your data is exactly why it can't leave.

Who writes it — and why that matters

Everything here is written from inside the problem, not adjacent to it. The author's background is the reason to trust the material:

Academic
PhD, Physics
IGZO and graphene semiconductor material properties — the physics under the devices a fab actually ships.
Industry
Process Engineer
Semiconductor / display deposition (EV evaporation). Real production lines, real yield, real constraints.
Applied AI
RL & Anomaly Detection
Reinforcement learning for process control and anomaly-detection systems built for the fab floor.
Focus
Air-gapped LLM
On-prem LLM serving, RAG over sealed documents, and agents wired to internal systems via MCP.

That combination — device physics, a working knowledge of the fab, and hands-on applied AI — is uncommon, and it's the whole point. Manufacturing-AI advice usually comes from people who understand the models but not the line, or the line but not the models. This archive sits in the overlap.

What you'll find here

  • On-Prem LLM — choosing open-weight models, licensing, GPU sizing, and serving stacks that never phone home.
  • RAG — chunking process documents, on-prem embeddings, vector stores, and evaluating retrieval without a labelled set.
  • Agents & MCP — connecting agents to MES and equipment tools safely, with guardrails and audit trails.
  • Python Stack — deep dives on the lang ecosystem (LangChain, LangGraph) and the rest of the production toolkit.
  • Manufacturing AI — the applied cases and first principles: virtual metrology, anomaly detection, and process control.

How it connects to the courses

The journal is the field-notes companion to the archive's interactive courses. Each article points to a matching deep-dive deck when you want the full, slide-by-slide treatment — the Fab AI masterclass, air-gapped LLM knowledge systems, and the three-part Enterprise LLM series.

Editorial stance. Content is written for practitioners — students, process engineers, and platform teams alike. It's bilingual (English first, Korean alongside), free to read, and carries no signup. Where a claim depends on a specific setup or vendor, we say so. We'd rather be useful than impressive.
